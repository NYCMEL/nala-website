# SEO Automation Options

## What NALA Can Automate

- Generate page titles, meta descriptions, canonical URLs, Open Graph tags, and local-business schema from the client's Business in a Box profile.
- Generate service pages and city/service-area copy from approved business data.
- Generate and update `sitemap.xml` for hosted client pages.
- Add structured review data from published first-party reviews where policy allows.
- Track indexability with Google Search Console URL Inspection once the site/property is verified.
- Submit URLs through sitemaps and Search Console workflows.

## Google

- Google Business Profile APIs can create and update business location data, posts, photos, service areas, and reviews at scale.
- They require OAuth/account access and, for new locations, business verification before listings appear on Google Search and Maps.
- Search Console APIs can inspect indexed status and query search performance, but they do not guarantee ranking or indexing.
- The Indexing API is not a general-purpose local-business page submission tool; use sitemaps and Search Console for normal pages.

## Other Local SEO Places

- Apple Business Connect can manage Apple Maps place cards. API access is mainly for approved third-party partners or delegated service accounts; many small businesses use the web portal.
- Bing Places can add, claim, and update listings shown in Bing Search and Bing Maps. It has verification and data-quality requirements.
- Yelp's public API is primarily for reading/matching Yelp business data and reviews; listing creation/claiming is not a simple public write API.
- Aggregators/listing platforms such as Yext, Uberall, SOCi, Reputation, Local Data Exchange, and similar vendors can push NAP data to many directories through one integration.
- Industry and citation targets worth tracking manually or through a listings provider: BBB, Angi, Thumbtack, Nextdoor, Yellow Pages, Chamber of Commerce, Facebook Pages, LinkedIn company pages, and locksmith/local-service directories.

## Practical Recommendation

NALA should automate on-site SEO immediately because the client sites are hosted here. For maps/directories, build the Business in a Box profile so it exports clean NAP/service data, then add integrations in this order: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect or a listings aggregator, then secondary citations.
