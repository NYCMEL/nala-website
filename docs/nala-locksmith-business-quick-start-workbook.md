# NALA Locksmith Business Quick Start Workbook

## Purpose

This workbook helps a new locksmith turn training, tools, and Business in a Box into a simple operating plan. It is not a replacement for hands-on training, legal advice, insurance advice, licensing rules, or local code requirements.

Use it to decide what services to offer first, how to answer calls, how to price common jobs, how to collect reviews, and how to stay organized during the first 30 days.

## Safety And Legal Checklist

Before accepting paid jobs, confirm:

- Your state, county, and city locksmith licensing requirements.
- Whether a business license, sales tax registration, or contractor registration is required.
- Whether your work requires insurance, bonding, or a background check.
- Which services you are trained and equipped to perform safely.
- Which services you will refuse until you have more training.

## The First Services To Offer

Start narrow. A new locksmith should avoid promising every possible service. Choose simple, repeatable jobs that match your training and tools.

Good starter services:

- Residential lockouts where lawful proof of access can be verified.
- Rekeying standard residential locks.
- Lock replacement for common residential door hardware.
- Basic lock installation and hardware swaps.
- Simple commercial rekeys after authorization is verified.
- Vehicle lockout only when you have the correct tools, training, and legal authorization.

Services to postpone until trained:

- Safes and vaults.
- High-security restricted key systems.
- Electronic access control.
- Automotive key programming.
- Forced entry on sensitive sites.
- Any job where ownership/authorization cannot be confirmed.

## Customer Authorization Script

Use a calm, professional script before dispatch:

"I can help. Before I begin any locksmith work, I need to verify that you are authorized to access the property or vehicle. Please have a photo ID ready, and if the address on the ID does not match, please have another proof of access such as a lease, registration, utility bill, work order, or manager authorization."

Do not proceed if authorization is unclear. Document the reason and leave politely.

Authorization notes:

| Job type | Proof to request | Do not proceed if |
|---|---|---|
| Home lockout | ID, lease, bill, owner/tenant proof | Customer cannot show connection to property |
| Vehicle lockout | ID, registration, insurance, rental agreement | Customer cannot show connection to vehicle |
| Commercial property | Manager/owner approval, work order, ID | No authorized contact approves work |
| Rekey | Owner/manager approval | Dispute or unclear authority |

## First 30 Days Plan

### Week 1: Setup

- Confirm licensing and legal requirements.
- Set up business phone and voicemail.
- Complete Business in a Box profile.
- Choose one business card design.
- Order starter cards and key tags.
- Publish the client website.
- Add business hours and service area.
- Create invoice and review workflow.

### Week 2: Local Presence

- Create or claim Google Business Profile if eligible.
- Add photos, logo, business description, services, and hours.
- Set up Bing Places and Apple Business Connect where possible.
- Create a Facebook business page.
- Ask friends or local contacts for referrals, not fake reviews.
- Visit local property managers, real estate agents, and small businesses.

### Week 3: First Jobs

- Track every call.
- Log every quote, won job, lost job, and reason.
- Send invoice immediately after each job.
- Let the automatic review request follow the invoice.
- Review pricing after the first few jobs.

### Week 4: Tighten Operations

- Identify the top two service types that convert.
- Improve phone script.
- Add photos of legitimate completed work.
- Create a simple referral offer.
- Reorder cards/key tags if useful.
- Set next 30-day revenue target.

## On-Site Job Checklist

- Park safely and professionally.
- Greet the customer.
- Verify authorization before work begins.
- Inspect the lock, door, frame, or vehicle.
- Confirm price before work starts.
- Take before/after photos if appropriate and permitted.
- Complete the work.
- Test the lock with the customer present.
- Clean up.
- Send invoice.
- Thank the customer and let the review request workflow run.

## Review Request Workflow

Reviews should be earned, not bought. Do not offer payment for reviews. Do not ask employees, relatives, or fake accounts to review the business.

Good closeout script:

"Thank you for trusting me with the job today. I just sent the invoice. You may also receive a review request. If you feel I did a good job, a short honest review helps a new local business a lot."

## Business Card And Key Tag Plan

Your business card and key tag should make one promise clearly:

- Who you are.
- What you do.
- Where you serve.
- How to call you quickly.
- Where to find your website.

Recommended first order:

- 200 business cards.
- 25 key tags for customers, property managers, and referral partners.

Reorder when:

- Fewer than 50 business cards remain.
- Fewer than 5 key tags remain.
- Phone number, website, service area, or business name changes.
